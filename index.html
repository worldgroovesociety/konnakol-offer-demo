Why rhythm slips

You can hear the groove. Locking into it is the hard part.

  • Most of the time it is not the notes that fail you. It is the time underneath them.
  • You rush the fast bars and drag the slow ones.
  • Odd meters in five and seven feel like guesswork.
  • A metronome shows you that you are off, never how to fix it.
  • Theory makes sense on paper, then disappears the moment you play.
  • You count in your head instead of feeling the pulse.
  • Solos come apart by the time the cycle comes back around.
Where groove really comes from

First you feel time in your body. Then you build a vocabulary for it.

Before technique, before theory, before anything, comes the internalization of time: feeling the pulse in your body instead of counting it in your head. After that comes vocabulary. Most musicians who lack groove are not short on feel, they are short on options, and rhythmic vocabulary is where the options come from. Konnakol is that vocabulary in its purest form, the rhythm of an entire tradition, spoken out loud.

Time in the body
